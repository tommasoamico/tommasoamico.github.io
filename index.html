I took my degree in September 2023 with a thesis on microbial scaling laws. Koln traffic regulator with Dask

This work starts from a project jointly developed by IBM and by the German city of Koln thought to be a first step towards traffic regulation and an efficient exploitation of transport's resources. The mobility is emulated with SUMO, an open-source traffic simulation suite which allows generating the movement around a predefined city road map, extracting the data for the analysis from past traffic scenarios.

With the aid of Dask, through some Cloud Veneto virtual machine we analyze the 20GB dataset to extract information before visualizing a live traffic map of the city of Koln highlighting stressed areas at different moments of the simulated period.

Single-cell lineages simulation with Haskell

During my master thesis, to study universality classes of single-cell lineages, simulations were needed. I found myself dealing with critical slowing down and thus, to reach convergence, very long lineages were key.
Although the first plain Python implementation worked fine I needed something more efficient. An Haskell implementation was thus developed, exploiting the notion of monad computations and specifically of the State Transformer monad The much needed speed up was achieved and the code was also made more readable exploiting the features of pure functional code provided by the Haskell language.

Skeletal bone age assessment

The project's goal was to correctly retrieve the correct skeletal bone age from X-ray images as labeled by medical experts. Data was collected by different U.S. hospitals and was made public in a challenge aimed at imporoving performances and algorithms on the subject.

Identifying streaks with entropy

Lisa Vittozzi is definitely one of the greatest italian biathlete in history. She faced a part of her career though where she struggled with prone shooting.
In the article we investigate her hiccups and als analyze the first part of Lisa's resurgency in the 2022/2023 season. Specifically we verify wether mistakes were trouly random or if some external factor e.g. psychological effect could have been the cause for the abovementioned stuggles. We achieve this with the aid of information theory and entropy concluding that poor performance did not seem to be originated from a technical deficiency but on the contrary that the psychological side played a role. We also observe that the early part of her resurgence does not evidence any anomalous behaviour,

The Relative Age Effect in biathlon

The Relative Age Effect (RAE) has been is a well known issue in several sports. In many instances there is indeed an imbalance favouring the athletes borned earlier in each age group. It is believed that differences in physical and psychological development that vanish in the long run are still very strong when children begin thei sporting career.
This differences cause problems in talent perception and motivation causing early drop-out among young athletes. We investigate wether the Relative Age Effect is present in biathlon taking into account the different recruitment periods of each country. A statistically significant inmbalance is found and discussed in the article.

Estimating win probability in Biathlon

In an effort to identify the greatest comeback and the most tragic collapses of the 2021/2022 season. We train a classifier tho estimate win probability and posium probability in Biathlon world cup races.
Probability is evaluated at each timed sector and at both ends of a shooting range. Several features are taken into account: not only athletes' static strenghts and shortcomings of but also considering form and performances close to the race considered.
Listing some findings we highlight the significance of a couple if comebacks like Marte Olsbu Røiseland's win over Hanna Sola and one of the many Quentin Fillon Maillet's win that season.
